The Problem

I needed a website that would present me — but I didn't know what it was supposed to look like. I lacked the vocabulary to describe what I wanted, and I didn't have the knowledge to understand what the AI was building.

Without framework

I spent hours writing precise commands, trying to control every detail — and still got generic outputs.

With framework

I defined the boundaries once. AI reasoned within them, pushed back when I contradicted myself, and proposed work I wouldn’t have thought to ask for.

The Framework

Five formal definitions — the rules every design decision had to pass. If an element didn't satisfy at least three, it was cut.

MinimalistEvery element serves the narrative. If it doesn't — deleted.
ClearNarrativeThe reader can follow without skipping. No confusion, no jumping ahead.
TextEmphasisWriting-led. Shows thinking. Rejects heavy graphics over prose.
AuthenticVoicePersonal, conversational, transparent about tools. Sounds like Talia — not corporate.
ProcessTransparencyShows methodology, exposes constraints, acknowledges AI use. Nothing hidden.
The World Model

Instead of guessing what the website should look like, I formalized the problem. I created a four-layer framework: definitions that constrain the design, decision logic that prioritizes clarity, execution within those boundaries, and a feedback loop to refine.

Five formal definitions. One decision rule. Every design choice tested against both. The result: 8 feedback cycles instead of 47.

Example From Our Conversation

I don't ask AI what to do. I tell AI what to think within.

Most people treat AI as a search engine or a shortcut. I treat it as a thinking partner with defined boundaries. I set the constraints. AI explores within them, proposes, and reasons. I approve or redirect. The result is work I couldn't have imagined alone — but that I fully own.

My Prompt

You're now operating in the world model. Start LAYER 1: DISCOVERY & DEFINITION. Research 3-4 successful portfolio websites. Propose initial definitions: Minimalist(portfolio), ClearNarrative(portfolio), TextEmphasis(portfolio)...

AI Response

AuthenticVoice(portfolio) — Your portfolio shows you're a person, not a professional template. Evidence: Brittany Chiang includes personal life. Kevin Powell admits uncomfortable truths. Pattern: Both humanize themselves. Why it matters: Authenticity = confidence. Humanization = connection. Personal voice = proves you're not AI-generated.

My Prompt

I have 2-3 projects. Visual: not text driven, something more interesting. Use decoration — animations and visuals. Hero section: suggest based on research. Pacing: a+c.

AI Response

Before I propose formulas, I need to reconcile something. Your definitions emphasize: Minimalist: ∀ element ∈ P → ServesNarrative(element). TextEmphasis: Text-driven. But you're asking for visuals, animations, decorations. Should I: A) Revise definitions? B) Interpret decoration within minimalism? C) Something else? What's your call?

What I Learned

I watched the AI build the site and learned to read the code. I debugged errors, corrected design choices I disagreed with, and edited text directly in the repository. I wasn't dependent on AI for changes — I could make them myself.

That independence matters. The framework gave AI direction, but I stayed in control. I learned enough to verify the output, to fix what needed fixing, and to iterate without waiting.

AI as a tool means understanding what it builds — not just accepting it.
